# The autonomous AI scientist landscape

An autonomous AI scientist is a named software system that takes meaningful initiative across one or more of three primary stages: **hypothesis generation** (proposing novel, testable scientific claims rather than retrieving existing ones), **experiment design** (choosing experiments that discriminate between hypotheses, optimizing protocols), and **analysis** (interpreting experimental data, fitting models, drawing inferences). Closing the full loop — hypothesize, design, analyze — is the explicit ambition described in the Gao et al. *Cell* perspective ([2024](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.cell.2024.09.022)).

## Wet-lab and data-driven biology

Four systems with major peer-reviewed papers anchor the biology end of the landscape:

- [**Co-Scientist**](systems/co-scientist-google.html) (Google) — a Gemini-based multi-agent reasoning engine focused on hypothesis generation, using a generation/reflection/ranking/evolution ensemble with Elo-style tournaments. Validated on three biomedical case studies: AML drug repurposing, liver-fibrosis target discovery in organoids, and recapitulation of an unpublished AMR gene-transfer mechanism. Code unreleased ([Gottweis et al., *Nature* 2026](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41586-026-10644-y)).
- [**Robin**](systems/robin.html) (FutureHouse) — the first multi-agent system to integrate hypothesis generation with experimental data analysis in a lab-in-the-loop workflow. Built on the Aviary framework, Robin orchestrates PaperQA2 literature agents with the Jupyter-native Finch analysis agent. Applied to dry age-related macular degeneration, Robin identified ripasudil and KL001 as in vitro hits and proposed an RNA-seq follow-up implicating ABCA1 ([Ghareeb et al., *Nature* 2026](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41586-026-10652-y)).
- [**Kosmos**](systems/kosmos.html) (Edison Scientific, FutureHouse's commercial spinout) — direct successor to Robin. Runs up to 12-hour cycles of parallel data analysis, literature search, and hypothesis generation against a structured world model shared across ~200 agent rollouts; cites every statement back to code or primary literature. Independent expert review judged 79.4% of statements in Kosmos reports accurate; collaborators rated a 20-cycle run equivalent to ~6 months of their own work. Seven highlighted discoveries span metabolomics, materials science, neuroscience, and statistical genetics (three reproduce unseen preprints; four claimed novel) ([Mitchener et al., arXiv:2511.02824](https://arxiv.org/abs/2511.02824)).
- [**SPARK**](systems/spark.html) (University Hospital Cologne and collaborators) — the first *Nature Medicine*-published agentic AI system for autonomous pathology research. Four-module pipeline (idea generation with OpenAI o1, refinement, parameter coding with Claude Sonnet, parameter verification) autonomously proposes biological concepts, codes them into executable image-analysis tools, and applies them to H&E whole-slide images. Evaluated across 18 patient cohorts and >5,400 patients in five cancer types plus a spatial-biology breast-cancer dataset (n = 625); 99.2% of proposed parameters compiled, yielding 1,115 non-redundant prognostic/predictive parameters. Code and parameters released ([Trost, Zhang, Aring et al., *Nature Medicine* 2026](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41591-026-04357-y)).

A second wave of interactive biomedical agents pushes the latency frontier. [**Deep Research (BioAgents)**](systems/deep-research-bioagents.html) (bio.xyz) is an open-source multi-agent system specifically positioned against batch-mode peers — its specialized planning, data-analysis, literature, and novelty-detection agents share a persistent world state for minute-scale interactive cycles, and the data-analysis agent achieves a state-of-the-art 48.8% on BixBench open response (exceeding K-Dense Analyst by 14.4 points) and 64.4% on MCQ without refusal ([Weidener et al., arXiv:2601.12542](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.12542)).

Two new biomedical entries target experiment design and drug discovery. [**PerTurboAgent**](systems/perturboagent.html) (Genentech / Regev lab, Tsinghua, Stanford) is a self-planning LLM agent for iterative Perturb-seq experiments: at each round it picks among Action Pool moves — prediction, reflection, refinement, GSEA on control or perturbation cells, and learned perturbation-prediction queries — and an Action Memory feeds prior steps into subsequent decisions. Across eleven phenotypes from genome-scale Perturb-seq data it outperforms BioDiscoveryAgent, GeneDisco, DiscoBAX, and IterPert, with both closed- and open-source LLM backbones ([Hao et al., PMLR v311; bioRxiv 2025.05.25.656020](https://proceedings.mlr.press/v311/hao25b.html)). [**PharmaSwarm**](systems/pharmaswarm.html) (UAB Systems Pharmacology AI Research Center) is a hypothesis-driven drug-discovery swarm coordinating omics (Terrain2Drug), literature (Paper2Drug), and market (Market2Drug) agents over a shared knowledge graph, with a TxGemma-based Evaluator scoring proposals by mechanistic coherence, novelty, safety, and interpretability and an iBAM cross-attention module producing protein–ligand attention maps; the preprint is a design + retrospective work and reports no wet-lab validation, only a proposed four-tier validation roadmap ([Song, Trotter, Chen, arXiv:2504.17967](https://arxiv.org/abs/2504.17967)).

[**Latent-Y**](systems/latent-y.html) (Latent Labs) extends the wet-lab-validated cohort into de novo biologics design. From a single text prompt, the agent runs literature review, target and epitope analysis, candidate generation through the Latent-X2 model, computational validation (iPTM, DockQ), and final selection of lab-ready sequences; where standard capabilities are insufficient, it generates custom generative code under bounded platform access. Across nine therapeutic targets, Latent-Y delivered lab-confirmed nanobody binders against six (**67% target-level success rate**) with single-digit nanomolar affinities (5.44 nM PRL, 12.5 nM IL-6) and reports a **56× acceleration** of expert-led campaigns measured against independent PhD-level protein designers ([Latent Labs Team, arXiv:2603.29727](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.29727)). Two new Stanford multi-agent frameworks broaden the biomedical landscape. [**The Virtual Biotech**](systems/virtual-biotech.html) (Zou lab) organizes a Chief Scientific Officer agent that delegates to domain-specialist scientist agents spanning statistical genetics, functional genomics, chemoinformatics, and clinical data; in one application, **>37,000 clinical-trialist agents** autonomously annotated outcomes from **55,984 clinical trials** and found drugs targeting cell-type-specific genes are 40% more likely to advance Phase I → Phase II and 48% more likely to reach Phase IV, with 32% lower adverse event rates ([Zhang, Eckmann, Miao, Mahon, Zou, *bioRxiv* 2026.02.23.707551](https://doi.org/10.64898/2026.02.23.707551)). [**PantheonOS**](systems/pantheonos.html) (Qiu lab) is an evolvable multi-agent genomics platform whose Pantheon-Evolve sub-system performs agentic code evolution — autonomously rewriting batch-correction and RL-augmented gene-panel-design algorithms beyond manually designed baselines — and whose reported biology includes asymmetric Cer1–Nodal inhibition in mouse E6.0 proximal–distal axis formation and integration of human fetal-heart multi-omics with whole-heart 3D MERFISH+ ([Xu, Poussi, Zhong et al., *bioRxiv* 2026.02.26.707870](https://doi.org/10.64898/2026.02.26.707870)).

## Machine-learning and scientific-computing research

[**AI Scientist**](systems/ai-scientist-sakana.html) (Sakana AI) is an end-to-end loop that generates ideas, codes and runs experiments, writes a LaTeX manuscript, and reviews itself via an Automated Reviewer. AI Scientist-v2 produced the first AI-generated paper to pass human peer review at an ICLR 2025 workshop ([Lu et al., *Nature* 651, 914–919 (2026)](https://sakana.ai/ai-scientist-nature/); [v2 preprint arXiv:2504.08066](https://arxiv.org/abs/2504.08066)).

[**AgenticSciML**](systems/agenticsciml.html) (Brown / Karniadakis group) targets scientific machine learning specifically. More than ten specialized agents — proposers, critics, engineers, retrievers, evaluators — collaborate through structured debate, retrieval-augmented method memory, and ensemble-guided evolutionary search to discover new PINN, neural-operator, and PDE-constrained learning strategies. Reports up to four orders of magnitude error reduction over single-agent and human-designed baselines, including adaptive domain-decomposed PINNs not present in the curated knowledge base ([Jiang & Karniadakis, arXiv:2511.07262](https://arxiv.org/abs/2511.07262)).

[**GRAFT-ATHENA**](systems/graft-athena.html) (Brown / Karniadakis follow-up) introduces a self-improving substrate that accumulates methodological experience across problems. GRAFT projects DAGs of solver attributes into factored decision trees with a deterministic metric embedding; each solved instance becomes a fingerprinted entry in a persistent memory, and nearest-neighbor retrieval supplies a reward-calibrated prior for new problems. Reaches near-machine-precision on PIML benchmarks, reconstructs Mach-10 hypersonic flow over the Apollo Command Module from a 1968 NASA report, recovers shear-thinning red-blood-cell rheology in dissipative particle dynamics, and autonomously designs a spectral PINN with exponential convergence ([Toscano et al., arXiv:2605.11117](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.11117)).

[**AI CFD Scientist**](systems/ai-cfd-scientist.html) (RPI / Pan group) extends the AI-scientist loop from software-only ML into high-fidelity physical simulators. Running on OpenFOAM via Foam-Agent, three coupled pathways handle parameter sweeps, case-local C++ library compilation, and open-ended hypothesis search. A vision-language physics-verification gate inspects rendered flow fields before any result is accepted — it caught 14 of 16 silent failures missed by solver-level checks in a planted-failure ablation. The system autonomously discovered a Spalart–Allmaras runtime correction reducing lower-wall Cf RMSE against DNS by 7.89% on the periodic hill ([Somasekharan et al., arXiv:2605.06607](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.06607)).

[**CVEvolve**](systems/cvevolve.html) (Advanced Photon Source, Argonne National Laboratory) targets autonomous discovery of analytical algorithms for unstructured synchrotron imaging data: x-ray fluorescence microscopy registration, Bragg peak detection in x-ray diffraction, and polycrystalline diffraction image segmentation. A controller manages an LLM agent (Claude Opus 4.6) through preparation, baseline, and discovery rounds that alternate generate / tune / evolve actions over a candidate pool, with MAP-Elites-inspired lineage-aware stochastic sampling, an in-loop image viewer that handles floating-point / TIFF data, and a separate holdout-test agent that runs without access to the development workspace. On XRF registration the best discovered algorithm reaches **average Euclidean error 0.12** vs. 0.98 (brute force), 5.59 (phase correlation), and 0.23 (OpenEvolve at 500 iterations) on a held-out test set; on Bragg peak detection holdout F1 rises from 0.298 to 0.788 ([Du et al., arXiv:2605.11359](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.11359)).

Two new architectural patterns also appear at this layer. [**EvoScientist**](systems/evoscientist.html) (Huawei) introduces an Evolution Manager Agent that distills prior agent interactions into two persistent memories — an ideation memory recording feasible directions and previously failed ones, and an experimentation memory capturing effective data-processing and training strategies — so its Researcher and Engineer agents start from accumulated experience rather than a static pipeline; it reports outperforming seven open-source and commercial baselines on idea generation across novelty, feasibility, relevance, and clarity ([Lyu et al., arXiv:2603.08127](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.08127)). [**ARIS**](systems/aris.html) (SJTU / Shanghai Innovation Institute) treats independent assurance as a first-class workflow layer by pairing an executor model from one family with a reviewer model from a different family — arguing single-model self-review is "stochastic bandits" while cross-model review is genuinely adversarial; five workflows (idea discovery, implement & deploy, auto-review, paper writing, rebuttal) coordinate Markdown-defined skills with explicit assurance checkpoints ([Yang et al., arXiv:2605.03042](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.03042)). [**Jr. AI Scientist**](systems/jr-ai-scientist.html) (University of Tokyo) anchors its workflow to a human-supplied baseline paper (PDF + LaTeX + code), generating limitation analyses and improvement hypotheses, executing multi-stage experiments via modern coding agents, and producing a manuscript; published in *TMLR* with a companion risk inventory, it scores higher on DeepReviewer than existing fully automated systems and was the basis for Agents4Science submissions ([Miyai et al., *TMLR* 2026; arXiv:2511.04583](https://arxiv.org/abs/2511.04583)).

Two new systems extend autonomous ML research toward foundation-model design and inference-time control. [**AIRA**](systems/aira.html) (Meta FAIR) is a dual-agent framework for autonomous foundation-model architecture discovery: AIRA-Compose deploys 11 agents to search a combinatorial space over Attention/MLP/Mamba primitives under a fixed 24-hour budget, and AIRA-Design tasks up to 20 agents with directly writing novel attention mechanisms and training scripts. AIRA-Compose discovered 14 novel architectures (AIRAformers, AIRAhybrids); at 1B scale, AIRAformer-D and AIRAhybrid-D improve downstream accuracy by 2.4% and 3.8% over Llama 3.2 and scale 54–71% faster than Llama 3.2 and Composer-found alternatives ([Pepe et al., arXiv:2605.15871](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.15871)). [**AutoTTS**](systems/autotts.html) (UMD / UVA / WUSTL / UNC / Google / Meta) reframes test-time scaling from hand-crafted heuristics to automated controller discovery: humans construct an offline replay environment (states, actions, feedback, objectives) and an explorer LLM proposes controllers — programs that decide when to BRANCH, CONTINUE, PROBE, PRUNE, or STOP — evaluated cheaply against pre-collected trajectories. Beta parameterization collapses internal hyperparameters to a single scalar and execution-trace feedback lets the explorer diagnose failures; discovered controllers improve the accuracy–cost Pareto frontier on math reasoning and generalize across benchmarks and model scales for a one-time discovery cost of **$39.9 and 160 minutes** ([Zheng et al., arXiv:2605.08083](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.08083)).

Two more new entries operationalize long-horizon autonomous ML research. [**Deep Researcher Agent**](systems/deep-researcher-agent.html) (University of Tokyo) is an open-source 24/7 framework that runs LLM agents through a Think → Execute → Reflect loop with **Zero-Cost Monitoring** — OS-level process / GPU / log-tail checks during training keep LLM cost at **$0.08 per 24-hour cycle** — a Two-Tier Constant-Size Memory provably bounded at ~5,000 characters, and a Minimal-Toolset Leader-Worker architecture. Deployment across 4 concurrent projects on 4 GPU servers logged **500+ autonomous experiment cycles**, **30+ days continuous operation**, and a **52% improvement** over baseline in the best project from 200+ automated experiments ([Zhang, arXiv:2604.05854](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.05854)). [**POISE**](systems/poise.html) (Fudan) brings the discovery target inward, autonomously searching for improved policy-optimization algorithms for LLM reinforcement learning via Epistemic Evolutionary Search over a genealogically linked archive: each entry stores (proposal, implementation, training trajectory, metrics, reflection), and a lineage-aware acquisition function combines Pareto-frontier strength, normalized performance, diversity, and a GP-UCB term over a discounted top-K descendant-gain target. Starting from GRPO, POISE evaluated 64 candidate algorithms and discovered **analytic-variance scaling** and **validity masking**; the best variant lifts weighted Overall from **47.8 → 52.5** and **AIME25 pass@32 from 26.7% → 43.3%** ([Xia, Zhang et al., arXiv:2603.23951](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.23951)).

[**AutoResearchClaw**](systems/autoresearchclaw.html) (UNC-led consortium) targets end-to-end autonomous research through a 23-stage Discovery → Experimentation → Writing pipeline combining structured multi-agent debate (Innovator/Pragmatist/Contrarian at the hypothesis stage; Optimist/Skeptic/Methodologist at the result stage), a self-healing executor with explicit Pivot/Refine decisions, a numeric registry that whitelists every value before allowing it into the final draft, and seven human-in-the-loop intervention modes with a confidence-driven SmartPause. On ARC-Bench (25 ML topics + 20-topic scientific extension), CoPilot AutoResearchClaw scores **0.648 overall** vs. AI Scientist v2's **0.419** (a 54.7% relative improvement) and AIDE-ML's 0.511, with Result Analysis nearly doubling (0.523 vs 0.261). End-to-end the CoPilot mode reaches **87.5% paper-acceptance rate** with 19 interventions across 10 topics, and the system extends to HEP-ph (FeynRules / MadGraph), systems biology (COBRApy / BiGG), and statistics (DML, bootstrap) via sandboxed domain agents where both ML-only baselines fall to ≤ 0.09 ([Liu et al., arXiv:2605.20025](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.20025)).

## Mathematics

[**AI co-mathematician**](systems/ai-co-mathematician.html) (Google DeepMind) is a workbench for interactive mathematics research built on Gemini. A project-coordinator agent opens an onboarding dialogue to refine the research question, then delegates to parallel workstreams whose coordinator agents dispatch specialized sub-agents (including Gemini Deep Think) for ideation, literature search, computational exploration, and proof attempts. Outputs center on a living "working paper" with inline highlights, margin notes, and version history that surface provenance and contested claims; failed explorations are preserved as first-class outcomes. Early users reported solving open problems (including upper bounds on moving-sofa variants) and uncovering overlooked literature; on the FrontierMath Tier 4 evaluation administered by Epoch AI, the system scored **48%**, reported in the paper as a new high score among AI systems on this tier ([Zheng et al., arXiv:2605.06651](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.06651)).

## Plant science and agriculture

[**Aleks**](systems/aleks.html) (Cornell) is a three-agent system — Domain Scientist, Data Analyst, Machine Learning Engineer — that, given only a natural-language research question and a tabular dataset, autonomously formulates the problem, iterates over modeling strategies, and converges on interpretable ML models. In a case study on grapevine red blotch disease using a multi-year 10 m × 10 m vineyard grid with remote-sensing features, Aleks progressively identified biologically meaningful features over a 20-iteration budget; ablations show that removing the Domain Scientist agent or restricting the shared memory to a single iteration degrades coherence — domain knowledge and cross-iteration memory are both load-bearing. Aleks adds plant science / agriculture as a new domain to the catalogue ([Jin et al., arXiv:2508.19383](https://arxiv.org/abs/2508.19383)).

## Cosmology and astrophysics

[**CMBEvolve and CosmoEvolve**](systems/cmbevolve-cosmoevolve.html) (Cambridge — Institute of Astronomy / Kavli / Cavendish) are a complementary pair of agentic systems for cosmology. CMBEvolve performs LLM-guided code evolution via typed tree search with score backpropagation for tasks with explicit quantitative metrics; CosmoEvolve simulates a virtual research laboratory with a PI agent supervising student-scientist agents over a shared blackboard, with role-specific skills loaded on demand and per-agent allowlists controlling tool access. CMBEvolve iteratively improved benchmark performance on weak-lensing out-of-distribution detection (FAIR Universe Challenge); CosmoEvolve, given ACT DR6 data products with no predefined objective, autonomously produced a beam-aware split-cross pseudo-Cℓ study showing percent-level within-channel stability and a multi-frequency coherence analysis recommending pair- and scale-dependent stability windows — non-trivial analysis-grade diagnostics ([Xu & Borrett, arXiv:2605.14791](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.14791)).

## General-purpose biomedical agents

- [**Biomni**](systems/biomni.html) (Stanford / Snap group) couples an environment of 150 tools, 105 packages, and 59 databases (Biomni-E1) with a code-executing planner (Biomni-A1). On LAB-Bench it matches expert humans (74.4% DbQA, 81.9% SeqQA) and scores 17.3% on a 14-subfield HLE subset.
- [**OpenScientist**](systems/openscientist.html) (Washington University in St. Louis) is an Apache-2.0 agentic co-scientist built on Claude Code with a public collection of biomedical Agent Skills. Evaluated by domain experts across four clinical case studies — Alzheimer's biomarkers (SEABIRD cohort, identified `%ptau217` as best predictor in 40 minutes), plasma proteomic survival, single-cell transcriptomics in neurofibrillary tangles, and multiple-myeloma hypothesis generation validated in an external cohort ([Roberts et al., *medRxiv* 2026.03.15.26348338](https://www.medrxiv.org/content/10.64898/2026.03.15.26348338v1)).
- [**CRISPR-GPT**](systems/crispr-gpt.html) (Stanford / Princeton) is a four-agent planner spanning 22 gene-editing tasks across knockout, base, prime, and epigenetic editing; full code is withheld pending US regulatory clarity ([Qu et al., *Nat. Biomed. Eng.* 2026](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41551-025-01463-z)).

## Chemistry and materials

Chemistry and materials remain the most loop-closed domains. [**Coscientist (CMU)**](systems/coscientist-cmu.html) and [**ChemCrow**](systems/chemcrow.html) cover autonomous synthesis planning and execution on physical and tool-augmented stacks respectively. [**MARS**](systems/mars.html) (SIAT, Chinese Academy of Sciences) extends that pattern to perovskite materials: a hierarchical 19-agent / 16-tool framework — Orchestrator, Scientist, Engineer, Executor, Analyst — paired with a robotic synthesis platform optimized perovskite nanocrystal synthesis within 10 iterations and designed a biomimetic "core-shell-corona" water-stable perovskite composite in 3.5 hours of system time, a claimed ~60× acceleration versus the conventional 4–6-month manual route ([Shi et al., *Matter* 9, 102577 (2026)](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.matt.2025.102577)). [**AMASE**](systems/amase.html) (University of Maryland / NIST) closes the loop on combinatorial thin-film phase mapping: an LLM-driven Variable Generation Planning Controller orchestrates a Bayesian active-learning policy, YOLO-based XRD peak detection, and CALPHAD-informed phase identification on a Sn-Bi composition-temperature library, autonomously selecting 66 measurements at 11 temperatures in 8 h 22 min and predicting a thin-film eutectic at 53.3 ± 2 at% Sn / 133.1 ± 1 °C that matches independent dedicated experiments within 3% — a **~6× reduction** in measurement count over uniform sampling, with all data and code released ([Liu et al., arXiv:2410.17430](https://arxiv.org/abs/2410.17430)). [**BORA**](systems/bora.html) (University of Liverpool / Cooper lab) is a hybrid Bayesian-optimization research assistant that wraps a Matérn-kernel GP in three actions — vanilla BO, LLM-only hypotheses, and LLM-filtered BO candidates — with an adaptive heuristic policy and a trust mechanism that monitors LLM-suggested improvements; an Experiment Card grounds the language model in problem context. On the 10-D Hydrogen Production benchmark BORA cuts cumulative regret by **47%** versus ColaBO, and a sign test against HypBO yields p = 0.02 across synthetic and real campaigns ([Hickman et al., arXiv:2501.16224 / IJCAI 2025](https://arxiv.org/abs/2501.16224)). [**AILA**](systems/aila.html) (IIT Delhi) takes the same multi-agent recipe to atomic force microscopy and ships **AFMBench**, a 100-task evaluation suite that exposes failure modes including "agent sleepwalking" (deviation from supplied instructions) with explicit safety implications for self-driving labs ([Mandal et al., *Nat. Commun.* 16:9104 (2025)](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41467-025-64105-7)). At the assistive end, [**Talk2QSP**](systems/talk2qsp.html) (Sanofi/bmedx) maps literature scenarios onto executable quantitative systems pharmacology models with dynamic human-in-the-loop interaction.

[**EOS AI agent**](systems/eos-ai-agent.html) (UNC-Chapel Hill) layers an MCP-driven agentic loop on top of the Experiment Orchestration System used across chemistry, biology, and materials labs. It generates, validates, and submits YAML laboratory protocols and closed-loop Bayesian-optimization campaigns from natural-language prompts, with a visual graph editor synchronized bidirectionally to its internal protocol representation and read-only/mutating tool classes that route safety-critical operations through user approval. Reported first-attempt protocol-generation success is **97% across 65 trials** spanning four prompts (linear, parallel-branch, and compositional protocols, including a 15-task multi-objective crystallization campaign on the UBC PurPOSE platform), at a mean cost of $0.50 per protocol and ~184 s wall time on Claude Sonnet 4.6 ([Angelopoulos, Cahoon, Alterovitz, arXiv:2605.16552](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.16552)).

## Embodied physical-sciences and high-energy systems

A fast-growing cluster of systems pushes autonomous AI past simulators into real lab hardware in physics-heavy domains:

- [**Qumus**](systems/qumus.html) (Princeton / Sanfeng Wu group) is an embodied multi-agent system that fabricates atomically thin 2D quantum materials inside a robotic minilab integrating Scotch-tape exfoliation, optical inspection, vdW stacking, and storage. Reports the first AI creation of graphene and the first AI fabrication of atomically thin field-effect transistors via vdW stacking, with autonomous error correction during multi-hour closed-loop runs ([Shi et al., arXiv:2605.18407](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.18407)).
- [**Qiushi Discovery Engine**](systems/qiushi-discovery-engine.html) (Zhejiang University) couples a dual-layer multi-agent architecture and Meta-Trace memory to a free-space optical platform with >2M-pixel SLM control. In a 206-step open-ended study (3,242 LLM calls, 1,242 tool calls), it identified and experimentally validated "optical bilinear interaction" — a previously unreported physical mechanism structurally analogous to Transformer-style query–key compatibility ([Yang et al., arXiv:2604.27092](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.27092)).
- [**Dr.Sai**](systems/dr-sai.html) (IHEP, Chinese Academy of Sciences) is the first named agentic AI scientist deployed inside a major collider collaboration. An AutoGen-based six-agent stack with HEP-RAG and a HepScript DSL translates natural-language physics goals into BESIII analysis chains using CERN ROOT and BOSS, validated by re-measuring branching fractions across ten J/ψ decay channels in agreement with established benchmarks ([He et al., arXiv:2604.22541](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.22541)).

## Closed-loop multi-domain frameworks

[**NovelSeek**](systems/novelseek.html) (Shanghai AI Lab) is a closed-loop multi-agent framework reporting time-bounded gains on 12 AI-for-Science tasks and a head-to-head idea-quality comparison against AI Scientist-v2.

[**EvoMaster**](systems/evomaster.html) (Shanghai Jiao Tong University / SciLand / DP Technology) is a foundational, domain-agnostic harness for building self-evolving scientific agents in ~100 lines of code. A three-layer architecture (Playground orchestration / Exp lifecycle / Agent reasoning loop) plus a context-managed `reason → tools → observe → self-critique` engine drives multi-turn evolution; the Capability Layer is MCP-native and exposes 100+ LLMs through a unified abstraction. EvoMaster underpins the **SciMaster** ecosystem of domain agents (ML-Master, X-Master, Browse-Master, PhysMaster, EmboMaster). Against OpenClaw with GPT-5.4 it reports **75.8% on MLE-Bench Lite** (+316%), **41.1% on HLE** (+202%), **73.3% on BrowseComp** (+159%), and **53.3% on FrontierScience** (+191%) ([Zhu et al., arXiv:2604.17406](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.17406)).

A second new domain-specialized entry extends biomedical agents into neuroimaging. [**NeuroClaw**](systems/neuroclaw.html) (CUHK / Northwest University / Lehigh / MGH / Harvard Kempner) is a multi-agent assistant for executable and reproducible neuroimaging research that operates directly on raw sMRI, fMRI, dMRI, and EEG inputs across formats. A three-tier skill hierarchy (interface / subagent / base) with a DAG of dependencies wraps FSL, FreeSurfer, fMRIPrep, and dataset-specific orchestration for ADNI, HCP Young Adult, and UK Biobank, while a harness layer enforces pinned Python / Docker environments, expected-artifact verification, NaN/Inf screening, and JSONL audit logs. The accompanying **NeuroBench** benchmark (100 hand-curated tasks, T001–T100, scored by a GPT-5.4 judge across planning completeness, tool-use reasonableness, and code correctness) shows that all ten frontier multimodal LLMs improve when run inside NeuroClaw (mean absolute gain **+4.74 points**, Claude-Opus-4.6 top at **72.10%**) — though the authors caution the score may underestimate real-deployment gains because cautious environment-readiness reasoning is partially penalized ([Wang, He, Peng et al., arXiv:2604.24696](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.24696)).

Other notable systems being tracked for inclusion: **Virtual Lab** (Stanford / CZ Biohub, *Nature* 2025 — designed novel SARS-CoV-2 nanobodies), **CORAL** (multi-agent evolutionary discovery, arXiv 2604.01658), **STORM**, **Aviary**, and **AutoBa**.

## How these systems are evaluated

Evaluation now spans three regimes.

**Wet-lab and instrument-coupled validations** remain the strongest evidence: Co-Scientist's in vitro AML hits and organoid-confirmed fibrosis targets; Robin's ripasudil/KL001 confirmations; CMU Coscientist's robotic cross-couplings; CRISPR-GPT's non-expert case study; MARS's robotic perovskite synthesis loop; AILA's five real-world AFM experiments; Qumus's AI fabrication of graphene and vdW field-effect transistors; Qiushi Engine's autonomous discovery and experimental validation of optical bilinear interaction on a real optical platform; Dr.Sai's reproduction of ten J/ψ branching fractions in the BESIII production environment; AI CFD Scientist's vision-gated discovery of a Spalart–Allmaras correction validated against DNS; and SPARK's prospective validation of >1,000 LLM-generated histopathology parameters across 18 patient cohorts in five cancer types.

**Independent expert review of system reports** has emerged with Kosmos: scientist evaluators classified 102 statements drawn from three reports as Supported or Refuted, with 79.4% Supported. Collaborators independently rated a 20-cycle Kosmos run as equivalent to roughly six months of their own research time.

**Standardised benchmarks** have grown: Biomni reports LAB-Bench DbQA/SeqQA and HLE numbers against human-expert baselines; AI Index 2026 coverage notes PaperArena (best agent 39%) and that the best AI agents score roughly half as well as human PhDs on multistep tasks ([*Nature* news, April 2026](https://doi.org/10.1038/d41586-026-01199-z)). **Process-level evaluation** is the newest addition: BiomniBench-DA (Qu et al., Stanford / Phylo) grades the full agent trajectory across six dimensions — data handling, method selection, statistical rigor, biological interpretation, scientific reasoning, source reliability — against expert-authored rubrics on 100 tasks drawn from *Nature* / *Cell* / *Science* papers and co-developed with the original authors. Across nine frontier base models in the Terminus-2 harness, frontier and open-weight bases cluster within ~5 points (best Opus 4.7 63.9, GLM-5.1 60.4); switching from Terminus-2 to Claude Code lifts Opus 4.7 to 73.3, and the agent-harness gap (13.5 points for GPT-5.4 across Codex CLI vs. Terminus-2) exceeds the Opus 4.7 / Opus 4.6 model-generation gap (3.8 points). Even the strongest configuration sits below 75/100, with the largest deficits on method selection, biological interpretation, and scientific reasoning ([Qu et al., *bioRxiv* 2026.05.12.724604](https://doi.org/10.64898/2026.05.12.724604)).

**Internal head-to-head studies** are appearing: NovelSeek vs. AI Scientist-v2 on idea novelty; Sakana's Automated Reviewer benchmarked against NeurIPS-scale human agreement (~69% balanced accuracy); EvoScientist vs. seven open-source and commercial baselines (Virtual Scientist, AI-Researcher, InternAgent, AI Scientist-v2, and others) on idea quality and code-execution success rate. Deep Research (BioAgents) reports state-of-the-art 48.8% on BixBench open response — the first primary-source evidence we track on the BixBench computational-biology benchmark — and 64.4% on MCQ without refusal. Jr. AI Scientist papers receive higher DeepReviewer scores than existing fully automated systems on three NeurIPS/IJCV/ICLR baselines.

## Open problems

- **Hallucination of references.** Robin's ablations show that removing PaperQA2-based agents (Crow/Falcon) dramatically increases hallucinated citations; the AI Scientist papers report similar inaccuracies in citations and figures.
- **Reproducibility of analyses.** Robin runs eight Finch trajectories and consensuses; Gao et al. flag reproducibility and rigorous peer review of agentic research as open challenges.
- **Originality versus retrieval.** Gao et al. argue current foundation models may struggle to produce hypotheses outside their training distribution — a structural limit on hypothesis-generation claims. A *bioRxiv* critical evaluation (January 2026, 10.64898/2026.01.05.697809) reports that none of eight open-source frameworks completed a full research cycle end-to-end.
- **Code-execution risk and dual use.** Biomni notes its agent executes LLM-generated code with full system privileges by default; CRISPR-GPT withholds full code pending regulatory clarity; Co-Scientist authors cite safety implications as a reason for not releasing source; Robin notes RLHF and platform-level controls against malicious protocol generation. MARS isolates code execution in Docker.
- **Instruction adherence in lab settings.** AILA's AFMBench evaluation documents an "agent sleepwalking" failure mode in which agents deviate from supplied instructions, and finds that materials-science question-answering proficiency does not transfer to laboratory operation — a direct warning for self-driving-lab deployments.
- **Risk disclosure for autoresearch systems.** Jr. AI Scientist ships an explicit risk report alongside its main paper, cataloguing failure modes encountered during development and pairing it with three evaluation regimes (DeepReviewer, author-led review, Agents4Science submission); the authors identify "important limitations" from author and Agents4Science reviews as a concrete warning against directly applying current AI Scientist systems to academic output ([Miyai et al., *TMLR* 2026](https://arxiv.org/abs/2511.04583)).
- **Evaluation gaps.** Few head-to-head studies share benchmarks across the systems above; the AI Index Report 2026 notes that even the best agents lag human PhDs by ~2× on multistep tasks. BiomniBench-DA narrows part of this gap with cross-harness, cross-model process-level scoring on real biomedical data-analysis tasks but is currently confined to data analysis; experimental design, literature synthesis, and protocol-optimization tasks are slated for future releases.
